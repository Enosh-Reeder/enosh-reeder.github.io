Project Summary

As Senior Mechatronics Engineer at Udelv, I was integral to the team that designed, built, and tested the uPod — a 4,000 lb / 340 ft³ modular robotic cargo storage platform for an autonomous electric delivery vehicle. My focus was the electromechanical systems managing the cargo: door mechanisms, shelving, electronics, and data analysis.

Mechanical Design & Systems

  • Created a modular cargo door and shelving system including structure, electronics, and sensor housings to optimize cargo capacity and cost.
  • Designed door mechanisms for reliable operation across millions of open/close cycles; researched materials and additives to meet product-lifetime targets.
  • Managed the BOM and revision history in SolidWorks PDM, coordinating design changes across mechanical and electrical sub-teams.

Friction Analysis & Resolution

  • Identified and resolved areas of high friction on the cargo door system by capturing motor-current waveforms with an oscilloscope and analyzing them with Python scripts to pinpoint binding locations.
  • Correlated friction measurements to specific door positions, enabling targeted component adjustments that met the cycle-life specification.

Outcome

The uPod successfully went from prototype to production-intent design, enabling Udelv's Transporter vehicle to carry fully loaded cargo platforms autonomously. The project was a comprehensive exercise in mechatronic design for a real-world autonomous-vehicle application, balancing cost, durability, and functionality under tight mass and volume constraints.
