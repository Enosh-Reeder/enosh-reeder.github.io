Project Summary

At QuantumScape, a company developing solid-state lithium-metal batteries, I implemented automated inspection and metrology systems for the cleanroom roll-to-roll electrode manufacturing line. The core challenge was achieving reliable defect detection on optically challenging thin-film materials at production line speeds.

Linescan Camera Systems

  • Deployed high-resolution linescan cameras and structured-light illumination across multiple stations on the roll-to-roll line to capture full-web imagery at speed.
  • Calibrated optics, lighting, and web-tension controls to minimize motion blur and achieve consistent contrast across reflective and translucent electrode materials.
  • Integrated cameras with PLC line-speed encoders for pixel-accurate image stitching and defect localization referenced to web coordinates.

Computer Vision Defect Detection

  • Developed a Python/OpenCV pipeline to automatically segment, classify, and log surface defects (pinholes, edge tears, coating voids) in real time.
  • Trained and validated a machine-learning classifier achieving >75% recall and >75% precision on held-out production data, validated against expert manual review.
  • Built a review dashboard that surfaced flagged regions to operators with annotated image crops, reducing manual review burden by more than half.

Dimensional Metrology Integration

  • Integrated laser-displacement sensors for real-time electrode thickness mapping, enabling SPC alerts before out-of-spec material reached downstream assembly.
  • Authored Python data-pipeline scripts to merge vision, thickness, and process telemetry into a unified per-roll quality record stored in a SQL database.
  • Worked closely with process and materials engineers to translate defect-taxonomy feedback into closed-loop process improvements.

Impact

The inspection system provided QuantumScape's production team with automated, quantitative quality data at every roll, enabling faster yield-loss root-cause analysis and accelerating the iteration cycle for their next-generation solid-state battery chemistry.
