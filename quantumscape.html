Project Summary

At QuantumScape — a company developing solid-state lithium-metal batteries — I worked as a Metrology Engineer on an R&D roll-to-roll slot-die coater for solid-state battery separators. The role combined precision optical system design, computer-vision pipeline development, and close collaboration with process and materials engineers to drive yield improvement.

Cleanroom Line-Scan Inspection Systems

  • Improved casting quality and yield on the R&D slot-die coater by identifying and addressing defect sources through automated optical inspection.
  • Designed and installed cleanroom optical line-scan inspection systems and web cleaners across the roll-to-roll line, capturing full-web imagery at production speeds.
  • Calibrated optics, lighting, and web-tension controls to achieve consistent contrast across optically challenging thin-film separator materials.
  • Integrated cameras with line-speed encoders for pixel-accurate image stitching and defect localization referenced to web coordinates.
  • Trained and validated computer-vision models for automated defect detection and segmentation, reaching >75% recall and precision — independently validated against Keyence VK microscopy and in-house metrology.
  • Evaluated X-ray and laser / 3D-microscope sensors as additional metrology modalities for the inspection line.
  • Used SQL for structured storage and querying of inspection logs, enabling yield-loss root-cause analysis across production runs.

Impact

The inspection system provided QuantumScape's production team with automated, quantitative quality data at every roll — enabling faster yield-loss root-cause analysis and accelerating the iteration cycle for their next-generation solid-state battery chemistry.
