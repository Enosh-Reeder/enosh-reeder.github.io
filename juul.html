Project Summary

On the internal tools & test team at JUUL Labs I design, build, and deploy automated systems that accelerate R&D throughput for aerosol-chemistry and device-reliability testing. The work spans robotics, embedded electronics, PCB design, analytical instrument integration, and data pipelines.

UR10e Robotic Work Cell

  • Developing a robotic work cell to automate device handling with a target of ~4× throughput improvement.
  • Python and ROS2/MoveIt control of a UR10e arm with an Intel RealSense depth camera and NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano for perception and edge compute.
  • Prototyped computer-vision and SLAM workflows on a Hiwonder JetHexa hexapod robot for algorithm development ahead of integration with the arm.

Other Projects at JUUL

Separate from the robotic arm work cell, I also lead a range of test, instrumentation, and production-tooling projects across the R&D organization.

Session-Data Capture & AI Analysis Pipeline

  • Built custom hardware integrating the device, a flow sensor, and a data logger to capture fully synchronized user-session data streams.
  • Built an AI pipeline that transcribes and analyzes session audio using cloud speech-to-text and LLMs, automatically correlating spoken user feedback with flow and zone-temperature data to inform firmware tuning decisions.

Aerosol Chemistry Instrumentation

  • Retrofitted and integrated an FTIR spectrometer for aerosol characterization — heated manifolds, custom inlets, solenoid puffing control, and a purpose-built enclosure.
  • Retrofitted and integrated a PI-TOF mass spectrometer (photo-ionization time-of-flight) for real-time aerosol composition analysis, including all mechanical and fluidic interfacing.
  • Brought toxicology testing in-house by building a 12-way solenoid-manifold test rig, cutting per-test cost by a six-figure margin annually.

Test Fixtures, PCBs & Production Tooling

  • Building an in-house clamshell FCT fixture (functional circuit tester) for validating new device mainboards, and a production-line QC tool that interfaces with an LCR meter for rapid impedance characterization of components.
  • Designed a magnetic pogo-pin charging/flashing dock and shipped approximately 150 units to external evaluation users (EUT).
  • Altium PCB designs: flexible RTD temperature-sensor board; rigid boards for flow measurement and USB-C integration.

Nitinol Actuator R&D

  • Designed superelastic nitinol actuators with manual and automated proof-of-concept prototypes for a new R&D project.
  • Characterized custom valves, pumps, and microfluidic components for integration into device test platforms.
