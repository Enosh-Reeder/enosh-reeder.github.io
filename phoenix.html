Project Summary

The Phoenix F2 is a purpose-built aerial firefighting aircraft designed from the ground up as part of the Cal Poly Aerospace Engineering capstone sequence (AERO 443/444/445). The design addresses the growing demand for next-generation air tankers capable of rapid response and high retardant capacity in challenging terrain. Completed with teammates Will Jo, Chino Cruz, Nathan Lee, and Jonathan Koutouan.

Key Specifications

Max Takeoff Weight59,400 lb
Wingspan93 ft
Retardant Capacity2,650 gal
Cruise Speed310 KTAS
Stall Speed64 KIAS
Range525 NMi
Wing Loading48 lb/ft²
CLmax3.4
PropulsionTwin Turboprop
ClassFAA Part 25

Design & Analysis

  • Performed aerodynamic sizing using XFLR5 and Star-CCM+ CFD to optimize wing planform and high-lift device configuration for low-speed drop-run performance.
  • Conducted structural trade studies on spar sizing and fuselage frames to meet FAA Part 25 load factors with minimum weight penalty.
  • Modeled the retardant delivery system and belly-door actuation mechanism in SolidWorks, validating drop-sequence timing against CAL FIRE operational requirements.
  • Completed full weight & balance, V-n diagram, and propulsion selection analysis for the twin-turboprop configuration.

Outcome

The Phoenix F2 design was presented at the Cal Poly AERO 445 design review, demonstrating a viable clean-sheet aircraft concept that meets FAA regulatory requirements and addresses real-world aerial firefighting mission profiles. The project sharpened full-aircraft systems-integration skills spanning aerodynamics, structures, propulsion, and avionics architecture.
